How Laser Hair Removal Works (And What to Expect)

If you’ve never had laser hair removal before, we understand that it might sound intimidating. But one of our jobs is to make the process easy to understand.

Here’s how it works: Laser hair removal uses focused light to target pigment in the hair follicle. The heat from the laser damages the follicle, slowing down future hair growth. Because hair grows in cycles, multiple sessions are needed to catch each hair in the right stage of growth.

At our Midland clinic, most clients need between 6 to 8 sessions, spaced several weeks apart, to see the best results. That’s why consistency is key — and we’ll work with your schedule to make it manageable.

During a session, the laser delivers quick pulses of energy to the treatment area. Some people compare the sensation to a rubber band snap, but many are surprised at how comfortable it feels, especially with our advanced cooling systems.

Afterward, you may notice mild redness or sensitivity, similar to a light sunburn. That usually goes away in a few hours. We’ll provide you with all the aftercare tips you need to keep your skin happy and healthy between visits.
